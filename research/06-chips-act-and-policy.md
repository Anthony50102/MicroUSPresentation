# CHIPS Act & Government Policy — Digital Twin Investment

## The CHIPS and Science Act — Overview

### What It Is
The **CHIPS and Science Act**, signed into law in August 2022, authorized approximately **$52.7 billion** to revitalize US semiconductor research, manufacturing, and R&D capacity.

### Funding Breakdown
| Category | Amount | Purpose |
|----------|--------|---------|
| Manufacturing Incentives | ~$39B | Subsidies for new fab construction in the US |
| R&D and Workforce | ~$13.2B | Research institutes, workforce training, digital twin R&D |
| NIST Programs | Included | Standards, metrology, testing infrastructure |

### Strategic Goals
- Reshore semiconductor manufacturing to the US
- Reduce dependence on Asian supply chains (especially Taiwan)
- Build advanced manufacturing R&D capabilities
- Train 100,000+ new semiconductor workers

## SMART USA Institute — The Digital Twin Centerpiece

### What It Was
The **SMART USA Institute** (Semiconductor Manufacturing and Advanced Research with Twins) was the CHIPS Act's flagship investment in digital twin technology for semiconductor manufacturing.

### Key Details
- **Funding**: $285 million from the Department of Commerce (CHIPS for America program)
- **Location**: Durham, North Carolina
- **Operator**: Semiconductor Research Corporation (SRC)
- **Announced**: November 2024
- **Partners**: National labs, universities, manufacturing groups, private industry
- **Goal**: Use digital twin technology to accelerate chip design, improve process yields, reduce costs, and train 100,000+ workers

### Scope
- Advanced chip design and simulation
- Packaging and testing optimization
- Manufacturing process digital twins
- Workforce development and training
- Wide-reaching industry collaboration

### Federal Agency Involvement
- US Department of Energy
- 10 Department of Energy National Laboratories
- Highlights the intersection of AI, digital twins, and microelectronics as a national priority

---

## The Setback: Funding Termination (December 2025)

### What Happened
In **December 2025**, the US Department of Commerce **terminated the $285 million contract** for the SMART USA Institute, halting its public-private digital twin R&D efforts.

### Why It Matters
- The center had been meeting initial goals
- Cut reflects shifting federal priorities / political leadership changes in 2025
- A significant blow to federally-funded digital twin research in semiconductors
- **IEEE Spectrum** covered this extensively as a major industry setback

### Implications
- Digital twin R&D in semiconductors will continue through **private funding** and industry partnerships
- Underscores the volatility of federal R&D investment in fast-moving political climates
- Companies like Samsung, TSMC, Intel continue investing independently
- University programs (Purdue/Dassault) continue with non-CHIPS funding

> Source: [IEEE Spectrum — Digital Twins Center Faces Funding Cut](https://spectrum.ieee.org/semiconductor-digital-twins-funding)

## Broader CHIPS Act Impact (Still Active)

Despite the SMART USA setback, the CHIPS Act continues to drive massive investment:

### Active Manufacturing Projects (28+ states)
| Company | Location | Investment | Status |
|---------|----------|------------|--------|
| **Intel** | Ohio, Arizona, New Mexico | $100B+ total | Under construction |

| **Samsung** | Taylor, Texas | $17B+ | Under construction |
| **Micron** | New York, Idaho | $100B+ total | Announced, progressing |

### R&D & Workforce Programs

- Advanced Packaging Manufacturing Program

- International technology security and innovation partnerships

## Presentation Narrative Opportunity

The SMART USA Institute story creates a compelling **narrative arc** for the presentation:

1. **The Promise**: Government recognizes digital twins as critical technology, invests $285M
2. **The Progress**: Institute launches, DOE and 10 national labs get involved, goals being met
3. **The Setback**: Funding terminated amid political changes
4. **The Lesson**: Digital twin technology is too important to depend on government alone — industry is moving forward regardless
5. **The Future**: Private investment, university partnerships, and industry consortia are the path forward

This is a **hot topic** for an audience at Microelectronics US — many attendees will have opinions about the CHIPS Act and its impact.

## Key Takeaways for Presentation

- The CHIPS Act represented the largest US investment in semiconductors in history
- A $285M digital twin institute was the R&D crown jewel — and then it was cancelled
- The cancellation is a VERY current, somewhat controversial topic (perfect for a talk)
- Despite the setback, the technology continues through private investment
- The broader CHIPS Act manufacturing investments continue and are transforming the US landscape
